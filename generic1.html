Your pain is the breaking of the shell that encloses your understanding—Kahlil Gibran

To understand how the Khalil Gibran series of videos was born, I proposed to create multimodal videoâ€™s using the conventional poetry/philosophy contained in The Prophet.

My idea was to digitally incorporate music, sound, voiceover, and images to a traditional text allowing for an expansion of meaning-making, engagement, and enjoyment for poetry possible, through the easily accessible online social media websites.

I wanted to create music, sounds, and images that align with the poems overall message, while deepening the sensory perceptions, eliciting emotions of the audience through atmosphere, tone, pitch, and tempo, etc.

My intention was to try to update the perception of poetry by incorporating current trending music genres can engage a new demographic of individuals in a more personable way. My intention was to parallel our individual need for self-expression, pushing past personal comfort-zones, and reimagining what is possible, outside of what we have always done, by throwing myself into this project as a beginner.

I believe poetry reading is intended to be grounded in feeling, instead of reading to understand, as most university courses require. Poetry seeks to confront, compel, and repel; or align, unite, and inspire. Either way I think poetry can be out of reach to some because it seems confusing or only about love. Yet, poetry is a form of self-expression, activism, and more; it is bound only by the constraints of the individual. I hope that I accomplished engaging the audience in a fun, approachable, and current way.
